Heaven Is My Country

client

Tristan Hsu

tags

Art Direction, Type Design, Branding

We were approached by filmmaker Tristan Hsu to create posters and title cards for his short film, Heaven Is My Country (2018). The film is a meditation on Asian-American identity, the supernatural, and interrogates what it means to be “Western.”

The title card features a re-interpretation of the typeface Rosewood, oft-used to quickly evoke “the wild west” due to its resemblance to old wooden types.

We created three versions of the poster, each featuring an abstracted figure — to reflect the film’s focus on mythology and character.
